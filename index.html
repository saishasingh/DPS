Introduction

Warmer air and intense heat lead to more frequent droughts, waves, floods & hurricanes across different regions

Shifts in temperature and rain alter the geographic of the plants and animal kingdom and the timing of the lifecycles leading to ecosystem imbalances and biodiversity loss.

The ocean absorbs a significant amount of carbon dioxide making it more acidic.

Economic Ripple Effects

Changes in weather patterns, water scarcity, & the spread of disease reduce crop yields directly impacting farmers life

Existing infrastructure from roads and bridges to power grids and water supply was not designed for extreme climate leading to costly damage and disruptions.

Heat waves make productive work more difficult.

MORE

Societal Impact

Climate driven declines in agricultural productivity water availability are major drivers of global hunger and malnutrition

Climate change increases health risk through various pathways including the vector borne diseases to new areas increased heat related illnesses and deaths

Extreme weather events and ongoing environmental degradations force millions of people to leave their homes often leading to migration & putting pressure on resources and existing social systems in new locations

MORE

Conclusion

The initial impact of climate change creates a complex web of interconnected problems that significantly shape the societies & the planet, highlighting the urgent need for global cooperation
